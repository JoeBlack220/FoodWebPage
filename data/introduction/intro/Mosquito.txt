Mosquitoes are a group of about 3500 species of small insects that are a type of fly (order Diptera).Mosquitoes have a slender segmented body, a pair of wings, three pairs of long hair-like legs, feathery antennae, and elongated mouthparts. Females of most species have tube-like mouthparts which can pierce the skin of the host  in order to extract blood, which contains protein and iron needed to produce eggs.
